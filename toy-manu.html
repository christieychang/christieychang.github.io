Toy Manufacturing

Interactive toys are crucial to a child's early education. Even simple toys such as a wind-up car toys or spinning tops can teach kids important motor skills and the fundamentals of how things work and move.

During my Rapid Prototype Design course, my team and I designed and built a manually-powered hand-crank xylophone with an attachable motorized part. Kids can attach small pegs onto a spinning barrel that plays the attached xylophone. They learn the basics of musical patterns by manually placing the pegs into the holes of the barrel and can observe how spinning the barrel at a certain speed plays the musical tune that they created.

With many different components to this toy, we learned how to problem-solve and think creatively to make our toy mechanism function well. We also gained invaluable experience in using tools like the laser cutter, drill press, bandsaw, belt sander, and drills, which are all useful in prototyping and building. After building a working hand-crank xylophone, we were able to test our prototype with kids and receiving feedback, which we used to improve our toy.
